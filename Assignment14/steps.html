LET'S GET STARTED!
SUPPLIES NEEDED:

  • Flowers! Can be any kind. The less bulky the receptacle (the part where the pedals and flower meet)
  • Two 4" x 4" square pieces of wood. Anything thicker than a 1/2" will do.
  • Two 4" x 4" pieces of card board
  • Two 4" x 4" pieces of paper
  • Two medium sized clamps
  • Plastic sleeve or small container to hold the pressed flowers once they are complete

RECOMMENDED EXTRAS:
  • Epoxy kit and silicone molds. If you would like to make jewelry or trinkets.
  • Glass sandwich frame. If you would like to create artwork you can hang!

SOME TIPS AND TRICKS:

  • Allow ATLEAST atleast 5 days for the flowers to be pressed. When in doubt, don't take it out!
  • The thinner the stem and receptacle, the better! More flat, that's where it is at!
  • If you use epoxy, make sure mixture is 50/50 and get those bubbles out. If you see bubbles, there are troubles!
  • When you pick the flowers, press right away. If you do not, you may lose your shot!

STEPS TO GET STARTED:

  1. Cut your flowers!
  2. place a piece of wood, cardboard, and paper combination. Items must be in that order.

STEPS CONTINUED...
  1. Place the other set of wood, cardboard, paper on top. Make sure it is even and everythin aligned.
  2. Take each clamp and clamp down on each side. Wait 5-7 days.
ALMOST THERE...

  1. YOU DID IT! take off the clamps and see your creations! Next you can use mold and epoxy to create jewerly or other trinkets!

Now you can check out samples and ideas!
